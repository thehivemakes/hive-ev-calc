How Temperature and Speed Affect EV Range

EPA range is tested under ideal conditions. Real-world range is affected by temperature (battery chemistry is less efficient in cold), speed (aerodynamic drag increases with the square of speed), and HVAC use.

At 20°F with heating on and highway driving at 70 mph, many EVs see 35–45% range reduction. Plan charging stops accordingly in winter.
